Methodology, indicator evidence & sources

Strongest historical predictors (ranked by documented hit rate):

1. Yield curve inversion (10Y–3M) — preceded every US recession since 1969, lead 6–24 months (avg ~14). Estrella–Mishkin (1996/98); used by NY Fed monthly. Caveat: 2022–24 inversion produced no recession — the model lets you discount it.

2. BIS credit-to-GDP gap — best single early-warning indicator of systemic banking crises (Borio–Lowe 2002; Drehmann–Juselius 2014). Gap > +10pp = Basel III warning threshold. US gap is currently negative — a key difference vs 2007.

3. Shiller CAPE — every reading >30 clustered around 1929, 1999, 2007, 2021 and now. Predicts poor 10-yr returns reliably, but NOT timing.

4. Margin debt / GDP & its YoY growth — peaked months before 1929, 2000, 2007, 2021 tops. YoY growth >50% has only occurred late-cycle (1999–2000, 2007, 2021, 2026).

5. HY credit spreads — sub-350bp = late-cycle complacency (mid-2007: ~250bp). The widening after the tights is the actual crash signal.

6. Buffett indicator, market concentration, Fed real-rate stance, capex/monetization gap — supporting context (Kindleberger–Minsky "displacement → credit boom → euphoria → distress → panic" cycle; Reinhart–Rogoff debt patterns).

Sources: NY Fed / Estrella–Mishkin; BIS credit-gap statistics; Shiller data; FINRA margin statistics; FRED (T10Y3M, HY OAS, fed funds); Advisor Perspectives dshort updates; BIS Quarterly Reviews.

⚠ This is a heuristic scoring framework, not a validated statistical model. No indicator set has ever predicted crisis timing with precision; the yield curve and credit gap give 6–24 month windows at best. I'm not a financial advisor — use for study, not trading.
